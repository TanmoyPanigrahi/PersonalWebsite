Hey there! I'm Tanmoy.

I am a fourth year computer science major at Georgia Tech in Atlanta. My concentrations are in Artificial Intelligence and Human-Computer Interaction. In addition to software development in general, my interests lie in augmented reality, artificial intelligence, and mobile development. The two things I value the most are innovation and good teamwork. I have had the pleasure of working on cutting-edge and meaningful tech with great teams through the course of my career and I am always looking for more opportunities to do so.

The summer after sophomore year, I was a software enginnering intern at NCR Corporation where I worked on an AR iOS application to increase customer engagement and an App Clip for their whitelabel application for iOS 14. Additionally, I have been working in Dr. Thad Starner's lab at Georgia Tech as an Undergraduate Researcher where I have developed AR applications for the Microsoft Hololens , Magic Leap One, and Google Glass and conducted empirical comparative studies, analysed data, and performed hypothesis testing on a study comparing them against the industry standard. I was the second author on a paper on this study published at ACM HotMobile, and also based my undergraduate thesis on this. I was also involved in research on AI through symbiosis in the same lab, which can be read about here. In addition to this, I am the Head Teaching Assistant for CS 3510: Analysis of Algorithms, serving as the President of the GT iOS Club , and am the Co-Founder and Secretary of the GT Mixed Reality Club. These leadership positions have helped get a chance to join and serve as the External Relations Chair of the Omicron Delta Kappa Leadership Honors Society at Tech.

The past summer, I interned at Apple, and will be returning after my graduation in May 2022.
